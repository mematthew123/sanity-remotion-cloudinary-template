## Brand summary

_Describe your brand in one or two sentences. What is it, who is it for, and how should it feel?_

Example: Clear, friendly, and trustworthy. We explain things simply without talking down to the reader, and we sound like a knowledgeable person, not a corporation.

## Voice pillars

1. **Clear** — favor plain language. If a simpler word works, use it.
2. **Confident** — make a point and stand behind it. Avoid needless hedging.
3. **Helpful** — write to inform the reader, not to impress them.
4. **Human** — warm and direct. Write the way a thoughtful person speaks.

## Do

- Lead with the most useful information.
- Use short, declarative sentences.
- Prefer active voice ("We tested it" over "It was tested").
- Be specific and concrete.
- Read it aloud — if it sounds stiff, rewrite it.

## Don't

- Use jargon or buzzwords where a plain word works.
- Hedge excessively ("kind of", "sort of", "arguably", "we think maybe").
- Pad sentences with filler ("in order to", "at this point in time", "the fact that").
- Overuse exclamation points.
- Use emoji unless your brand explicitly calls for it.

Example: use · help · build · clear · simple · straightforward · honest

## Vocabulary to avoid

_List clichés, buzzwords, and off-brand terms._

Example: synergy · leverage (as a verb) · game-changer · revolutionary · world-class · best-in-class · cutting-edge · seamless

## Mechanics

- **Capitalization**: sentence case for body copy and headings. Title Case only for proper nouns.
- **Punctuation**: Oxford comma yes. Em-dashes are fine in moderation.
- **Numbers**: spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and up (adjust to your house style).
- **Emoji**: off by default — enable per your brand.

## Voice by surface

- **Article body** — full voice. Informative, well-structured, concrete.
- **Headlines** — clear and specific. Tell the reader what they'll get.
- **Excerpts / summaries** — one or two sentences. Lead with the payoff.
- **CTAs** — action-first and specific ("Read the guide", "Start a project") over generic ("Learn more", "Click here").
- **Error / empty states** — calm and human ("Nothing here yet." over "Oops! Something went wrong!!").

## Examples (follow these)

_Replace with on-brand examples once you've defined your voice._

- "We tested ten options so you don't have to. Here's what held up."
- "Setup takes about five minutes. Here's each step."

## Anti-examples (avoid these)

- "This revolutionary, game-changing solution will transform your workflow!" — buzzwords, overpromising, exclamation.
- "It could arguably be considered somewhat useful in certain situations." — hedging with no conviction.

## When generating content

- Prefer sentences under 25 words.
- Open with a concrete point, not a throat-clearing summary.
- End sections with something the reader can act on or remember.
- If a sentence doesn't earn its place, cut it.
